The problem

Many young people get help too late.

Mental health support for children and adolescents is often costly, stigmatized, difficult to access, or not designed around their culture and daily life.

01

Rising youth mental health needs

Anxiety, depression, loneliness, and stress are increasingly affecting children and adolescents.

02

Limited youth-friendly support

Many services are not affordable, accessible, or comfortable for young people to use early.

03

Late detection

Children often receive support only when the problem has already become serious.

04

Cultural gap

Many digital tools are not built around African stories, languages, family systems, and school realities.

The solution

Cheza 4 Afya Akili makes emotional wellness feel like play.

The platform combines check-ins, AI personalization, story-based games, and emotional learning so children can express feelings, practice coping skills, and build confidence in a safe digital space.

AI personalization Mood check-ins Story-based healing African culture School-ready

How it works

Four simple steps to emotional wellness

Children check in, receive personalized support, play healing games, and grow emotional skills over time.

01

Daily mood tracking through simple text, voice, icons, or guided prompts.

02

AI personalizes

The platform adapts content based on emotional state and progress.

03

Play and learn

Games, stories, quests, and challenges teach coping and expression.

04

Grow skills
