Why Cheza Exists

Children in Africa face real mental health challenges

Many children and adolescents in Africa face stress, anxiety, bullying, trauma and emotional challenges with limited access to age-appropriate support. Cheza4Afya Akili is designed to close that gap.

Healing Games

Play-based healing adventures for young minds

Separate, age-appropriate experiences for children and adolescents — each rooted in African storytelling and evidence-based mental wellness.

💛
Children 6–12

Emotion Explorer Adventures

Learn feelings through characters and simple stories that build emotional literacy in a fun, safe world.

🌬️
Children 6–12

Calm Down Quests

Breathing and calming mini-games that help children manage worry, anger and anxiety step by step.

🏫
Children 6–12

School Life Missions

Safe scenario games that help children practise decisions around school, friendship and bullying.

🎙️
All Users

Safe Voice Stories

AI turns feelings into stories, helping users express emotions when words alone feel difficult.

🧭
Teens 13–19

Identity Quest Lines

Life storytelling journeys that help adolescents explore self-worth, identity and future goals.

🤝
Teens 13–19

Peer Healing Circles

Anonymous group healing missions that reduce isolation and encourage healthy peer support.

Who We Serve

We are here for the whole village

Cheza4Afya Akili is designed for every person who plays a role in a young person's wellbeing — from the child at the centre to the systems that surround them.

✅ Built FOR Africa, not imported to Africa

✅ Mobile-first — works on low-bandwidth devices

Scalability

From East Africa to the Global South

Starting where the need is clearest, then expanding thoughtfully to serve the whole continent and beyond.

1

Kenya, Tanzania & Rwanda

Build the foundation in East Africa — three countries, three languages, one shared platform.

2

East + West Africa

Expand to West African markets, adding French and more local languages as we grow.

3

Global South

Take healing through play to India, Latin America and every community that needs it.
